SachBol Review Guide

Comprehensive guidelines for effective misinformation detection and crisis response

Source Verification Protocol

Primary Source Checklist

  • Official Statements: Government agencies, health organizations (WHO, CDC), emergency services
  • Reputable News: Associated Press, Reuters, established news networks with fact-checking departments
  • Academic Sources: Peer-reviewed journals, university research, scientific publications
  • Cross-Reference: Verify across at least 3 independent reliable sources

Red Flags for Unreliable Sources

  • No author attribution or anonymous sources
  • Extreme emotional language or sensational headlines
  • Lack of publication date or recent updates
  • No contact information or about page

Crisis Response Protocol

Immediate Action Steps

  • Priority Escalation: Crisis claims jump to front of review queue automatically
  • Time Limit: All crisis claims must be reviewed within 30 minutes
  • Coordination: Notify crisis response team for high-severity misinformation
  • Template Responses: Use pre-approved correction templates for common crisis misinformation

Crisis Severity Levels

HIGH SEVERITY

Claims that could cause immediate harm, panic, or interfere with emergency response

  • False evacuation orders
  • Fake health treatments
  • Misleading casualty numbers

MEDIUM SEVERITY

Misleading information that could cause confusion or unnecessary fear

  • Exaggerated threats
  • Outdated information
  • Misattributed quotes

LOW SEVERITY

Minor inaccuracies or opinion-based statements

  • Speculative analysis
  • Editorial content
  • Personal opinions

Decision Making Framework

Verification Confidence Levels

  • High Confidence (Verified): Multiple reliable sources confirm, official statements support
  • Medium Confidence (Partially True): Some evidence supports, but details may be exaggerated
  • Low Confidence (False): Contradicted by reliable sources, lacks evidence, known misinformation pattern

Documentation Requirements

  • Brief reasoning note for all disputed AI assessments
  • List of sources consulted during verification
  • Timestamp and reviewer identification
  • Any additional context or mitigating factors
